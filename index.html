Teaching Method 3: Count On Strategy! ğŸ“

For Parents and Teachers: Teach the efficient "count on" method for addition.

Why Count On?

Instead of counting from 1 every time, we start with the bigger number and count on. This saves time and builds number sense!

Example: For 7 + 3, start at 7 and count "8, 9, 10" instead of counting all the way from 1.

Step-by-Step Method

1. Find the bigger number: "Which is bigger, 6 or 2? Circle the 6!"

2. Put it in your head: "Put 6 in your head" (tap your head)

3. Count on: "Now count on 2 more: 7, 8!"

4. The answer: "6 + 2 = 8"

Teaching Tips

âœ“ Use fingers to help count on

âœ“ Practice identifying the bigger number first

âœ“ Start with adding 1, 2, or 3 to larger numbers

âœ“ Make it physical: "Put 8 in your head, now count on your fingers"

âœ“ Celebrate when they remember to start with the bigger number!

Common Challenges

If your child always starts counting from 1:

â€¢ Practice just identifying bigger numbers first

â€¢ Use a number line to show the starting point

â€¢ Say "Don't start at 1, start at the bigger number!"

â€¢ Model the strategy repeatedly with enthusiasm

Teaching Method 4: Number Line Jumps! ğŸ“

For Parents and Teachers: Help children visualize math using number lines.

Why Use Number Lines?

Number lines help children see numbers as positions and movements. This builds a strong foundation for understanding math concepts visually.

Key idea: Addition means jumping forward, subtraction means jumping backward.

Step-by-Step Method

For Addition (5 + 3):

1. Find 5 on the number line and point to it

2. Jump forward 3 spaces: "6, 7, 8"

3. Where you land is your answer: 8


For Subtraction (7 - 2):

1. Find 7 on the number line and point to it

2. Jump backward 2 spaces: "6, 5"

3. Where you land is your answer: 5

Making It Physical

âœ“ Draw number lines on the floor with chalk

âœ“ Let children physically hop on the numbers

âœ“ Use toy cars or animals to "drive" along the line

âœ“ Point with your finger and make jumping sounds

âœ“ Count out loud with each jump

Teaching Method 6: Making 10 Strategy! ğŸ“

For Parents and Teachers: Teach the powerful "make 10" strategy for addition.

Why Make 10?

Making 10 first makes addition much easier! Children learn to break apart numbers to create friendly tens, then add what's left.

Example: 8 + 5 â†’ 8 + 2 + 3 â†’ 10 + 3 = 13

Step-by-Step Teaching

For 8 + 5:

1. "8 needs 2 more to make 10"

2. "Take 2 from the 5: 5 = 2 + 3"

3. "Now we have 8 + 2 + 3"
